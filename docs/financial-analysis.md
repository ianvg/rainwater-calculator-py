# Financial analysis

The **Financial analysis** tab provides a selected-tank simple-payback estimate based on the latest hydraulic simulation. Unlike the original ROI spreadsheet workflow, the application does not ask the user to multiply demand by an estimated tank efficiency. It calculates delivered rainwater as simulated demand minus simulated unmet rainwater demand and averages the delivered volume across the modeled calendar years.

Enter the currency label, tariff billing unit, water and sewer rates, installed cost, incentives or rebates, fixed annual maintenance, annual maintenance as a percentage of installed cost, and analysis period. Each demand object controls whether its delivered rainwater is eligible for sewer-charge savings; irrigation objects default to sewer-exempt. **Legacy aggregate demand eligible for sewer savings** is retained only for aggregate demand and demand objects migrated from older projects.

The tab reports average annual rainwater supplied, annual sewer-eligible rainwater supplied, avoided municipal-water charges, avoided sewer charges, their combined gross annual utility savings, annual maintenance, net annual savings, net installed cost after incentives, simple payback, and undiscounted net benefit over the analysis period. Water savings use all delivered rainwater; sewer savings use only delivered rainwater allocated to eligible end uses. Payback displays **Not achieved** when net annual savings are zero or negative. The same calculation is applied to every reliability-curve candidate in **Results > Candidate performance**; its financial cells remain blank until at least one financial rate or cost is configured.

This first release uses simple flat tariffs. It excludes tariff tiers, escalation, financing, discount rates, pump energy, equipment replacement, net present value, and internal rate of return.
